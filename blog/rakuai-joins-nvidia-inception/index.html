RakuAI joins NVIDIA Inception. Here is what we are doing with it.

May 31, 2026·Kevin Griffin

This week NVIDIA accepted RakuAI into the NVIDIA Inception program. It is a free program for AI startups - we are not paying for it, and NVIDIA does not own equity in RakuAI - but it unlocks four things that meaningfully change the timeline for our spatial pipeline: NGC catalog access, partner cloud credits, the Deep Learning Institute, and the global network of more than 19,000 AI startups already inside.

The honest version of why this matters: we are bringing NVIDIA-accelerated 3D Gaussian splatting to Raku Capture, the part of RakuAI that turns a phone scan of a room into an explorable splat. Today that pipeline runs on CPU - real COLMAP on real Azure Container Apps, producing real sparse point clouds in about twenty minutes. With NVIDIA-accelerated splat training (the Brush trainer on A10G / L40S), the same scan should land an explorable splat in two to three minutes. That is the difference between "interesting tech demo" and "consumer-grade capture experience". Inception is how a side-project-shaped startup affords to make that jump without burning runway.

What Inception actually gives a startup

The four things we care about:

  • NGC catalog. Pre-built containers for CUDA, RAPIDS (we will use cuDF for spatial queries), Triton, and the building blocks of a Brush trainer. Free, automatic for members. We replace the recon worker's base image with an NGC one and skip several days of dependency wrangling.
  • Partner cloud credits. Up to $5,000 in Microsoft Azure Cloud Credits, $100K in AWS Activate, and $150K in Nebius AI credits. The Azure piece is the right size for proving out Phase F2 on the same Azure stack the rest of RakuAI runs on. AWS and Nebius become the long-term GPU budget once we know what we are scaling.
  • Deep Learning Institute. Self-paced courses on fundamentals of accelerated computing with CUDA, RAPIDS for accelerated data science, and Triton for model serving. We will burn through the relevant three or four.
  • Networking and brand assets. The Member badge that now lives in the footer of every page on rakuai.com. Curated VC introductions. The kind of relationships that compound over a year.

The exact piece this unblocks

If you read our earlier post about the team-of-agents architecture, you know we have been treating reconstruction as a back-end detail that AI assistants drive through MCP. The capture pipeline that runs today is honest about what it is: COLMAP on a CPU. It produces a sparse point cloud and the viewer renders it the same way Brush eventually will. The orchestration is real. The geometry is not the geometry we want yet.

What is missing for "this is the consumer product" is the second stage: dense 3D Gaussian splat training on GPU. Brush is the open-source trainer we plan to base it on. A10G or L40S is the target architecture. Without Inception, that lane is a $4-7K/month line item before we have proven anyone wants the consumer product. With Inception, we can prove it inside the credit envelope - end-to-end - and only commit to ongoing GPU spend if iPhone users actually scan rooms and come back for more.

What changes in the product this month

Three things, in order:

  1. The NGC base image lands in the recon worker. The Dockerfile for raku-recon will swap from python:3.12-slim to nvcr.io/nvidia/cuda:12.4.0-runtime-ubuntu22.04. Side effect: even the CPU path becomes faster because we get NVIDIA's BLAS routines for free.
  2. A real GPU VM comes up in the East US 2 region. The Azure quota for NCads_A10_v4 already came through (A10 24 GB). We bring up one instance, install the Brush trainer, and switch RAKU_RECON_BACKEND over for opted-in users. The CPU path stays for everyone else until the GPU path is proven.
  3. The iPhone capture PWA stops apologizing. Today the upload screen says "this prototype" and the reconstruction takes long enough that users have to come back. Once Brush is wired up, the reconstruction completes while the user is still standing in the room they scanned. Whole different feel.

The honesty disclaimer that NVIDIA Inception requires

NVIDIA Inception is a free program for AI startups. RakuAI's membership does not imply that NVIDIA endorses RakuAI's products, nor that we are affiliated with NVIDIA beyond the program itself. The brand badge that now appears on rakuai.com is the official Member badge we are entitled to use under the brand portal's guidelines; we have not altered it, recolored it, or paired it with the NVIDIA corporate logo.

Try the current pipeline today

Scan a room yourself at rakuai.com/capture-app/ - iPhone Safari or Android Chrome, no install. The reconstruction you get back will be the CPU version. By the time the GPU lane lands, you will be one of the first to compare them.

If you are building something spatial and want to compare notes on running the same scan through a Brush variant you have wired up, mail partners@rakuai.com. We will be at Microsoft Build next week - NVIDIA is a partner there - if you would rather catch us in person.
