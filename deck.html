The pipeline

GPU-accelerated reconstruction

The pipeline is GPU-bound the whole way, from camera-pose recovery through splat training.

60–120s
→
GLOMAP / FastMap
Camera-pose recovery (CUDA)
30–90s

Brush
Gaussian-splat training (wgpu / Vulkan)
2–6 min
→
splat-transform
Compress to .spz (CPU)
5–15s

Target GPUs: L40S, A10, A100. Target time-to-splat is under 8 minutes per scan, at a cost under $0.50 per reconstruction.

The constraint

The bottleneck is GPU compute

Every other layer of RakuAI is solved or cheap. Reconstruction GPUs are the one line-item that gates real output.

Today

  • SimulatedBackend on prod
  • No real .spz served
  • Metric scale stays a documented seam
  • Users see placeholder geometry

With GPU credits

  • GlomapBrushBackend live on prod
  • A real Gaussian-splat .spz per scan
  • Real metric scale, accurate to the centimeter
  • LLMs query true room geometry

Unit-economics target is $0.20 to $0.50 of GPU cost per reconstruction. Investment pays for multi-cloud scale-out and the team to ship it.
